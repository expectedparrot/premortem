# Failure Statement

The failure statement is the most important sentence in the exercise.

It must state failure as a completed fact. Avoid hedging words such as `might`,
`could`, `possible`, or `risk`.

It should describe outcomes and consequences, not causes. Do not use causal
phrases such as `because`, `due to`, or `caused by`.

Good failure statements include vivid consequences: lost users, partner exits,
budget overruns, reputational damage, missed deadlines, or shutdown decisions.

Bad:

`The program might fail because stakeholders do not align.`

Good:

`The program was shut down after two years. Enrollment collapsed, two anchor
partners walked away, and the initiative became a cautionary tale internally.`
